# Code of Conduct

entropyx is a small project. The rules are small too.

## Be useful

Ship work that advances the question "what changed and what broke?"
being answerable. Show your reasoning. Attach evidence. Prefer a
repro over an opinion.

## Be honest

Don't claim a feature does what it doesn't. Don't soften a bug
report to protect feelings. If you find a broken claim in the
README, file it — we fixed two that way already.

## Be respectful

Criticize work, not people. Assume the other person is as smart as
you and was solving a problem you haven't seen yet. Ask before you
accuse.

## Enforcement

The maintainer (DJ, `dj@codetestcode.io`) reserves the right to
close issues, block comments, or revert contributions that violate
the spirit of the above. There's no formal committee. If you think
a call was wrong, email the maintainer directly and say so.

This is a working project, not a governance experiment. When the
project grows large enough to need a longer CoC, we'll adopt the
Contributor Covenant. Until then, the rule is: **make the instrument
more honest.**
